- No online/laptop processing; all processing must be done on board. 
    

- Modules are the full arm and the full robot base. 
    

- Each module can work independently; the robot base can be controlled via the app/controller, and the arm can do any random movement. 
    

- You can have one battery to power both modules or have two batteries for each module. 
    

- You can select the cubes to pick with your controller (ex: I moved the robot manually to face a cube; I then click on my app/controller a button to trigger the automatic arm movement to pick the cube) 
    

- Direct wheel connection to motor shafts is allowed, but you must calculate the minimum shaft diameter. 
    

- Omni wheels and Mecanum wheels are allowed. 
    

- When you reach the end after successfully placing RGB cubes (1 each), you can pick up the robot with your hands, switch it to manual mode, place it on the start, and restart again to get the bonus marks. 
    

- Obstacle avoidance will be done in manual and autonomous mode; the shape of the obstacle will be known at the day of the competition.
